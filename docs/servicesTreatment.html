Who I Work With

Stephen offers psychological support to individuals across the lifespan and welcomes people from all walks of life. His approach is grounded in compassion, curiosity, and deep respect for diversity—including alternative relationship structures, gender and sexual identities, and non-traditional lifestyles.

He specialises in working with:
  • Adults navigating life transitions, relationship challenges, trauma, or emotional distress
  • Parents and carers looking to better support their children's mental health and development
  • Children and adolescents experiencing anxiety, low mood, self-harm, or behavioural difficulties
  • Families seeking support around autism, ADHD, or other neurodevelopmental needs

Over the course of his career, Stephen has worked with a wide range of individuals and groups. Whether you're seeking support for yourself, your child, or your family, you can expect a collaborative, non-judgmental space that honours your values, strengths, and goals.

Stephen integrates multiple evidence-based approaches to meet each client's unique needs. His therapeutic methods include:

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)

A widely researched therapy proven effective for anxiety disorders, depression, PTSD, and OCD. CBT helps identify unhelpful thought patterns, challenge them, and replace them with healthier ways of thinking and behaving. Particularly effective for treating OCD.

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)

A structured therapy specifically designed to help process and resolve traumatic memories through bilateral stimulation techniques. EMDR reduces trauma symptoms while enhancing self-esteem and resilience. Especially effective for single-incident traumas, with a blended approach used for complex trauma.

Internal Family Systems (IFS)

An innovative approach that views the mind as composed of distinct "parts," each with unique feelings and roles. Effective for trauma, emotional regulation, and self-esteem issues, IFS fosters greater self-compassion by addressing wounded parts and nurturing the "Self."

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

Promotes acceptance and psychological flexibility to manage difficult thoughts and emotions. Based in mindfulness and behavioral change principles, ACT helps clients focus on their values and take aligned actions. Particularly helpful for anxiety, depression, chronic stress, and life transitions.

Compassion-Focused Therapy (CFT)

Designed for individuals struggling with self-criticism, shame, and emotional pain. CFT cultivates self-compassion and emotional resilience by integrating mindfulness, neuroscience, and evolutionary psychology to foster safety and self-kindness.

Psychodynamic Psychotherapy

Explores unconscious influences of past relationships and experiences on current behaviors and emotions. Helpful for understanding relational patterns, identity struggles, and unresolved emotional pain, building more meaningful connections with self and others.

Treatment plans are tailored to each individual's specific needs, often integrating multiple approaches for the most effective outcomes. Stephen works collaboratively with clients to determine the most appropriate therapeutic methods for their unique circumstances.
